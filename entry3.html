The effects of cubing on me







Cubing was a big part of my life for a good four years. I learned a lot about what I could do and my limits, and I even inspired a couple of other people. I think that it's most profound effect on me was my realization that anything was possible, as long as I tried. While I do not solve Rubik's cube-esque puzzles any more, I am still fond of any sort of logic puzzle. In general, I think it helped me to become a more well-rounded person in general. Without the problem solving skills I gained, I wouldn't be able to do many things I can now, or be as capable of a person. For instance, this web page. Everything you see on this (with the exception of the pin icon) was created and coded by me and a friend. After being inspired by said friend (Jack Engle), I decided to teach myself to program, and here we are now. However, I use my problem solving skills everywhere I go, not just in coding. I use them in school. I use them in scouts. I use them every day in my life.
